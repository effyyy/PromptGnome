# Code of Conduct

PromptGnome is a small project built around trust. We want this repository to be a place where anyone can ask questions, file issues, and share feedback without worrying about how they will be treated. This document explains what we expect from everyone who participates here, including maintainers.

## Our commitment

We want everyone to feel welcome regardless of experience level, background, identity, language, or location. Whether you are a security researcher filing a careful vulnerability report, a non-technical user describing a confusing bug, or a developer suggesting a feature, your contribution matters and will be treated with respect.

## What we expect

When participating in issues, discussions, and any other space connected to this repository, please:

- Be kind and patient. Assume the person on the other end is doing their best.
- Keep feedback focused on the work, not the person.
- Use plain, welcoming language. Not everyone reading along is a native English speaker or a developer.
- Accept that maintainers and other contributors may disagree with you, and that disagreement is not the same as disrespect.
- Give people room to make mistakes and to learn.

## What we ask you to avoid

The following behaviors are not welcome in this repository:

- Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning comments
- Harassment of any kind, in public or in private
- Sharing someone else's personal information without their clear permission
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwanted sexual attention
- Sustained disruption of conversations or deliberate intimidation
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a collaborative project

## Handling of personal data in issues

PromptGnome is a privacy tool, and that means we take particular care with what ends up in this public repository. Maintainers will edit or delete any issue that appears to contain real personal data (names, emails, account identifiers, payment details, anything sensitive), with a brief note explaining the action. This protects both the original reporter and anyone whose data may have been included by mistake.

Issues used for spam, abuse, or unrelated support requests will be closed and locked.

## Reporting a problem

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code of conduct, please contact the maintainers privately at **contact@promptgnome.com**. Reports will be handled with discretion. We will not share your identity with the person you are reporting without your consent, except where we are legally required to do so.

When you report an issue, please include as much context as you are comfortable sharing: what happened, where it happened, who was involved, and any links or screenshots that might help us understand the situation.

## How we respond

Maintainers will review every report we receive. Depending on the situation, our response may include any of the following:

- A private conversation with the people involved
- A request to edit or remove specific content
- A temporary or permanent ban from participating in this repository
- Escalation to GitHub if the behavior violates their terms of service

We aim to respond to reports within seven days. We will let the reporter know what action we have taken, to the extent we can do so without compromising anyone's privacy.

## Scope

This code applies to all spaces connected to this repository, including issues, discussions, pull request comments, and any direct communication that arises from project activity.

## A final note

We are a small team and we will not always get this perfect. If you think we have handled something poorly, please tell us. We would rather hear about it and improve than discover later that someone walked away unhappy.
